Orientation and Aspect

North and north-east are the best orientations.  North with views is even better.  West facing apartments with sun-control can also be fine.  South facing apartments are the only real no-no.  You should get some sunlight - it's absolutely critical.  East-west apartments have the possibility of east-facing bedrooms to catch morning light and west-facing living area for afternoon sun.  They also offer through-ventilation.  One should also consider whes are always increasing.  Wait is your proximity to other buildings?  Are you overlooking or overlooked?  Units with attributes like a view or a terrace will always be in more demand.  When the market is buoyant they will see above-normal growth.

Amenities

If an apartment has a car space or garage, make sure it is on the title.  If a unit has exclusive use of a terrace, make sure it is noted in the strata bylaws.  There is a hundred people to argue with if anything goes wrong.  Check if there is visitor parking.  Next best - is it easy to park nearby? It is always better if there is more than one lift in the building - and will your furniture fit into it? Also, a new apartment should have Internet cabling and finally, make sure the security system is easy to operate and makes you feel safe.

Words of Wisdom

The Department of Fair Trading has received complaints about many developers/builders.  "They all have been the subject of consumer complaints relating to misrepresentation, the provision of defective goods, over-charging and failure to provide goods", it says.  

The Dept recommends getting good legal advice, particularly when buying off the plan.  Get the lawyer to check the purchaser's right to rescind or reduce the salse price if promised works that vary considerably. Lawyers should also check what provisions there are if work is delayed and/or defective.  A certificate of home warranty insurance must be at their aspect (outlook) is.  Will you always have a view?  Particularly in inner-city areas where densitiprovided to purchasers with contract of sale.  An important difference when buying off the plan is that the developer should obtain an exemption so that the certificate of insurance can be provided at a later stage ( but within 14 days of issue).  A building inspection is also recommeded as good consumer protection.

Be aware that, with off-the-plan developments, there can be a clause transferring purchaser's voting rights for future management of the building.
